Who are we?

AraSci was formed under our ambition to surpass our own potential through creative thinking skills using the universal languages of coding and art. The name comes from the guarani word Arasy (mother of the sky) and the English word Science, representing our spirit to use scientific models and predictions to allow the DSCOVR to protect the Earth from geomagnetic storms, as a mother would. We were first brought into the Challenge by our Mechatronics teacher who encouraged us to put our learned programming skills into practice. At first, each of us was going to develop their project on their own, until, days before the deadline, we realized that each had different talents that, if combined, could fulfill the Challenge’s full range. This way, from people that are used to solo work, the DSCOVR initiative inspired us to embrace each of our diverse skills that all fit perfectly into the goal of our project. We ended up broadening our team to more members who complemented the original objectives through graphic design and editing skills. AraSci intends to promote collaboration to ultimately improve humans’ lives by securing a precise forecasting model that prevents the downfall of GPS satellite systems and electrical power grids.
